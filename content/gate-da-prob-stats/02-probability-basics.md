## Q101

[MCQ]

A fair die is rolled once. What is the size of the sample space $S$?

- **(A)** 1
- **(B)** 3
- **(C)** 6
- **(D)** 12

**Answer:** C

---

## Q102

[NAT]

Two fair coins are tossed simultaneously. How many elements are in the sample space?

**Answer:** 4

---

## Q103

[MCQ]

A card is drawn at random from a standard 52-card deck. Let $A$ = event that the card is a King, and $B$ = event that the card is a Heart. How many outcomes are in $A \cup B$?

- **(A)** 13
- **(B)** 16
- **(C)** 17
- **(D)** 4

**Answer:** B

---

## Q104

[MCQ]

For any two events $A$ and $B$ in a sample space, $P(A \cup B)$ equals:

- **(A)** $P(A) + P(B)$
- **(B)** $P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$
- **(C)** $P(A) \cdot P(B)$
- **(D)** $P(A) + P(B) + P(A \cap B)$

**Answer:** B

---

## Q105

[NAT]

For two events $A$ and $B$, $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B) = 0.5$, and $P(A \cap B) = 0.2$. What is $P(A \cup B)$?

**Answer:** 0.7

## Q106

[MCQ]

If $P(A) = 0.35$, what is $P(A^c)$?

- **(A)** 0.35
- **(B)** 0.65
- **(C)** 0.70
- **(D)** 1.35

**Answer:** B

## Q107

[MSQ]

Two events $A$ and $B$ are mutually exclusive. Which of the following must be true? (Select all that apply)

- **(A)** $P(A \cap B) = 0$
- **(B)** $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$
- **(C)** $A$ and $B$ are independent
- **(D)** $P(A) + P(B) \leq 1$

**Answer:** A, B, D

## Q108

[MCQ]

A bag contains 4 red, 3 blue, and 5 green balls. One ball is drawn at random. What is the probability that it is blue?

- **(A)** $\dfrac{1}{4}$
- **(B)** $\dfrac{3}{12}$
- **(C)** $\dfrac{5}{12}$
- **(D)** $\dfrac{1}{3}$

**Answer:** B

## Q109

[NAT]

A fair die is rolled. Using the classical probability formula, what is $P(\text{even number})$? Express your answer as a decimal.
