## Q607

[NAT]

The ordered dataset is $\{3, 7, 8, 10, 14, 18, 22, 25\}$. What is the IQR (= Q3 $-$ Q1)?

Q1 = median of lower half $\{3, 7, 8, 10\}$ = 7.5; Q3 = median of upper half $\{14, 18, 22, 25\}$ = 20. IQR = 20 $-$ 7.5 = 12.5.

**Answer:** 12.5

## Q608

[MCQ]

The Pearson correlation coefficient $r$ between $X$ and $Y$ is defined as:

- **(A)** $r = \dfrac{\text{Cov}(X, Y)}{\sigma_X \cdot \sigma_Y}$
- **(B)** $r = \text{Cov}(X, Y) \cdot \sigma_X \cdot \sigma_Y$
- **(C)** $r = \dfrac{\sigma_X \cdot \sigma_Y}{\text{Cov}(X, Y)}$
- **(D)** $r = \dfrac{\text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y)}{2}$

**Answer:** A

## Q609

[NAT]

Given $n = 4$ data pairs: $(1,2), (2,4), (3,5), (4,8)$. Compute $\text{Cov}(X, Y)$:

$$\bar{X}=2.5,\ \bar{Y}=4.75$$
$$\text{Cov}(X,Y)=\frac{(1-2.5)(2-4.75)+(2-2.5)(4-4.75)+(3-2.5)(5-4.75)+(4-2.5)(8-4.75)}{4}$$
$$=\frac{(-1.5)(-2.75)+(-0.5)(-0.75)+(0.5)(0.25)+(1.5)(3.25)}{4}=\frac{4.125+0.375+0.125+4.875}{4}=\frac{9.5}{4}=2.375$$

**Answer:** 2.375
